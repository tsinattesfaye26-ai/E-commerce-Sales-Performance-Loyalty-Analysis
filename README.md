# 📋 Project Overview
This project analyzes a dataset of 10,000 unique transactions to identify key drivers of revenue, customer loyalty, and operational efficiency. By leveraging SQL for deep-dive querying and Python for behavioral patterns, I have answered 18+ critical business questions to provide a roadmap for inventory optimization and marketing strategy.

# 🚀 Key Features & Insights
### 1. Customer Demographics & Behavior
Segmented Profiling: Analyzed spending habits across Age, Gender, and Location to identify the "High-Value" core customer.
Loyalty Tracking: Utilized Previous_Purchases data to benchmark customer retention and identify at-risk segments.
Subscription Impact: Measured the revenue delta between subscribers and non-subscribers to evaluate the current loyalty program.

### 2. Product & Visual Intelligence
Bestseller Discovery: Identified top-performing Items and Categories contributing to 80% of total revenue.
Attribute Popularity: Mapped demand for specific Size and Color combinations to inform stock replenishment.
Seasonality Analysis: Detected peak sales windows (Winter, Summer, Fall, Spring) to assist in seasonal marketing campaigns.
### 3. Logistics & Promotional Strategy
Shipping Optimization: Evaluated the popularity of Shipping_Types (Express, Standard, Free) vs. total order value.
Discount Efficiency: Measured the actual ROI of Promo_Codes and Discounts on incremental sales volume.
Payment & Frequency: Analyzed Payment_Methods and return-frequency (Monthly/Quarterly) to predict cash flow.

# 🛠️ Technical Stack
SQL (MySQL): Data cleaning, advanced joins, and KPI aggregations.
Python (Pandas/Matplotlib): Outlier detection, statistical distribution, and trend visualization.
Power BI: Executive-level interactive dashboards for stakeholder reporting.
Documentation: Professional PDF summary sheets focusing on actionable business recommendations.

### 📊 Sample Results
Revenue Leader: The Clothing category generated the highest income, specifically in the Fall season.

Top Customer Segment: Active subscribers (45% of total) spend 30% more per transaction than non-subscribers.

Geographic Focus: Montana and California lead in transaction volume, representing primary target markets.
